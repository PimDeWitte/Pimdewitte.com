Pim de Witte

I'm a tech entrepreneur, philanthropist, software developer, and investor.

About me

I'm an entrepreneur from The Netherlands and I currently live in Miami. I'm just a normal guy who loves programming, writing, learning, thinking, snowboarding, travelling and socializing. I'm passionate about entrepreneurship, philanthropy, technology, and business. I'm engaged to Rachel and I run the creative team at C2 Miami.

How it all started

When I was little I was diagnosed with Tourette's syndrome. My escape from tics was video games, and when I was 12 years old I started a YouTube channel with gaming videos. The channel quickly became a success, reaching over 50 thousand subscribers. This journey lasted for about 2 years.

Making videos was a great introduction for me to what computers were capable of, and the beauty of creating and building things. Videos have since then always been a crucial part of my life.

Soulsplit

At 14, I decided to build my own game instead of creating videos about other games. It quickly became a large success and it turned into a multi-million dollar business where I was fortunate to manage a team of over 30 brilliant team members. Our customers came from everywhere, and we have lots of fun stories to tell. I decided to leave this company in September 2014 and move to the USA.

Whitespell

Whitespell builds amazing tech products and then provides NGOs with a team, funding, and expertise for technology projects. We're the founders of Project Buendia, an effort to help improve efficiency using tech in Ebola treatment centers in West Africa. This project was eventually taken over by Google.
